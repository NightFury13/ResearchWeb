Results

The problem with the results in the previous experiments is resolved! The output of the l2_loss layer of the model seemed to be glitchy. I simply took the output images (output of last decode layer - decode1) and computed the euclidean distance between the original images and the adversarial images created. The results now seem to be much more intuitive but do not really form a decision boundary between the raw images and their corresponding adversaries.

The following are the average reconstruction errors for the complete MNIST (60000 images) on the autoencoder trained in previous experiments. Adversarial images have only a little more error than their corresponding raw images.
