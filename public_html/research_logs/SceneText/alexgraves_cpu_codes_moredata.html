Improvement in Scenetext performance with raw-features

Improvement and Insights into AlexGraves CPU codes

Feeding in more data greatly helps improving the accuracies for Scenetext. Instead of the 10-15k training samples from the previous experiments, I used 50k and 120k training samples for this experiment and the results are much better as compared to previous findings.

Previous experiments conducted by Minesh used 300k samples and normalised-features instead of raw-features and that gets an accuracy of ~65%. Looks like a good area to explore. 

Results : Oxford English Scene-Text (50 epochs, ~1548sec/epoch) [Train : ~50k images, Test : ~5k images, Val : ~5k images]

Train Errors:
  ctcMlError 23.0263		[This is the CTC error]
  Label-Error 76.91%		[This is the edit-distance between prediction and target]
  Seq-Error 99.98%		[Constant throughout -- No sequence was completely predicted correctly]

Validation Errors:
  ctcMlError 22.7519 
  Label-Error 76.28% 		
  Seq-Error 99.98%
